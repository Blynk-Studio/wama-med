## Design Context

### Users

**Primary — Moroccan families in crisis:** A family in Casablanca has just received a complex multi-specialist diagnosis. They are overwhelmed, scared, and logistically lost. They don't know which doctor to call first, which clinic accepts their insurance, or whether the cardiologist and neurologist will ever speak to each other. They are visiting this site at a moment of high anxiety, using their phone, probably at night.

**Secondary — Diaspora Moroccains:** Moroccan mothers and fathers living in France, Belgium, or the Netherlands who are trying to manage a parent's care in Casablanca from 2,000km away. Their fear is compounded by distance. They need someone on the ground with authority and knowledge.

**Tertiary — International patients:** Sub-Saharan African patients needing cardiac/surgical procedures unavailable at home. European medical tourists seeking quality orthopedic or surgical care at 40-60% of European costs.

**The job to be done:** Find someone trustworthy to take over the navigation of a complex medical situation. Confirm they are real, expert, and reachable. Contact them before the fear gets worse.

**Emotional state at arrival:** Overwhelmed, scared, skeptical (they've been let down by medical systems before), and cautiously hopeful that this could be the answer.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Warm authority arriving.

**The metaphor:** A riad in Casablanca — a traditional Moroccan house that appears plain from the street but opens onto an ordered, beautiful, calm courtyard. The medical system is the chaotic street outside. Wama Med is the riad gate. Once inside: clarity, care, expert navigation.

**Voice:** A brilliant, experienced friend who happens to know every specialist in Morocco — not a call center, not a corporate institution, not an emergency service. Expert and warm simultaneously.

**Emotional goal:** The visitor should feel relief before they read a single sentence. The design itself should communicate "someone expert is already here, and they've prepared for your arrival."

**Anti-patterns:**
- Clinical cold (stethoscope stock photos, blue-on-white, institutional fonts)
- Emergency urgency (not a crisis service — a premium coordination partner)
- Corporate distance (no faceless logos, no anonymous "our team")
- Generic French professional (not a law firm, not a bank)

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual theme:** Mediterranean luxury restraint. The Moroccan riad. Filtered light through geometric screens. Warmth without excess. Authority without coldness.

**Palette:**
- `#0B4042` — Deep algae-teal (primary brand color, hero backgrounds, CTAs)
- `#F5F0E4` — Warm limestone (section backgrounds, card surfaces)
- `#B8903A` — Warm brass (accents, icons, hover states, number counters)
- `#FAFAF8` — Warm white (maximum legibility sections)
- `#1C1410` — Deep warm ink (headline type)

**Typography:**
- Display: Fraunces (variable axis serif — literary, warm, French-heritage feel, aged elegance)
- Body: DM Sans (clean, modern, excellent French diacritics, neutral authority)
- Accent: Crimson Pro italic (patient voices, founder quotes, emotional pullquotes)

**Hero animation:** Canvas mashrabiya — slow-rotating geometric light pattern (Moroccan carved screen inspiration) in warm gold on deep teal. Responds subtly to cursor movement. Communicates: order from chaos, filtered light, Moroccan heritage, calm intelligence.

**References:**
- Solace Health (proof-first conversion, forest green warmth) — concept, not aesthetic
- Clinique Nescens (luxury through restraint, space as message) — spacing philosophy
- NO REFERENCE to any previous Blynk build (AGC/Carter/Gihan/Bana DDS)

**Mode:** Primarily light (limestone/white sections) with deep teal hero and closing CTA sections.

### Design Principles

1. **Calm before the call.** Every design decision should reduce anxiety before it increases conversion intent. Relief first, then action. Proof before ask.

2. **Warmth is the differentiator.** Every competitor is clinical cold. Wama Med's warmth — in color, typography, photography, and copy tone — is the primary competitive advantage. Never let a section feel clinical.

3. **The riad metaphor governs.** Sections flow like rooms in a riad: each has its purpose, its character, its light. The progression from hero (the gate) → services (the courtyard) → founder (the host) → process (the tour) → contact (the invitation) should feel like being received, not sold to.

4. **French literary precision.** The copy is in French. The design should honor French literary tradition: generous white space, elegant type, restrained decoration. The French is not a translation — it IS the voice.

5. **Authority through organization.** Wama Med's product is organization. The site must feel radically more organized than any competitor's — cleaner layout, better hierarchy, more deliberate spacing, more intentional type scale. The design IS the product demo.
